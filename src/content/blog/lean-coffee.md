Lean Coffee is a structured, but agenda-less meeting format. Participants gather, build an agenda, and begin talking. Conversations are directed and productive because the group decides what to talk about.

## How it works

1. **Set up a board** with three columns: To Discuss, Discussing, and Discussed.
2. **Generate topics**: everyone writes topics they want to discuss on sticky notes (physical or virtual).
3. **Vote**: each person gets a set number of votes (usually two or three) to place on the topics they find most valuable.
4. **Discuss**: start with the highest-voted topic. Set a timer (usually five minutes). When the timer goes off, the group votes on whether to continue or move on.
5. **Repeat** until time runs out.

## Why it works

Lean Coffee respects everyone's time. The democratic voting ensures the group discusses what matters most to them, not what a single person has decided is important.

It is particularly effective for:

- Team retrospectives
- Community of practice sessions
- Informal knowledge sharing
- Breaking the ice with new teams

## Tips for remote sessions

- Use a virtual whiteboard tool (Miro, Mural, or even a shared spreadsheet)
- Keep the timer visible to everyone
- Nominate a facilitator to manage transitions
- Allow a moment of silence for topic generation before anyone shares
