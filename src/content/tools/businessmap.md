## Why Businessmap?

Most project management tools treat Kanban as a feature. Businessmap treats it as a philosophy.
Where other tools bolt on a board view as an afterthought, Businessmap is built from the ground
up around flow-based delivery.

## What makes it different

Businessmap (formerly Kanbanize) provides genuine portfolio-level visibility. You can see
initiatives flowing through a strategic workflow while the cards underneath follow their own
delivery workflow. This hierarchy means leadership gets the view they need without micromanaging
the teams doing the work.

### Flow metrics that matter

Built-in cycle time charts, throughput analysis, and ageing work item indicators mean you do not
need a separate analytics tool to understand your delivery performance. The data is right there,
attached to the work itself.

### WIP management done properly

WIP limits are first-class citizens, not an afterthought. You can set limits at the column
level, the lane level, or the person level. The system makes overloading visible, which is
exactly what you want if you are trying to improve flow.

## Who it suits

Businessmap is best for teams and organisations that are serious about Kanban and flow-based
delivery. If you just need a simple task list, it is more than you need. If you want to
genuinely manage work in progress, improve cycle times, and make data-driven delivery decisions,
it is excellent.

## smagile's experience

We have used Businessmap daily since 2024 for all client delivery, internal product development,
and strategic planning. It handles everything from high-level initiative tracking to granular
task management across multiple teams and projects.
